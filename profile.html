Runner Bio

I'm a born runner. As a small child I learned to walk exceptionally early and promptly followed that up with other physical activites such as talking, climbing, and playing with toys. As was befitting of someone with such keen physicality I joined the military at the age of 17 and learned how to run properly, the army way. Focused on aspects of running movement usually ignored by amateur runners this particular philosophy emphasised running while keeping pace and cadence with hundreds of other people, not breathing too much, and wearing the exact type and quantity of clothing required.

Unsurprisingly this was not enjoyable, and after leaving an environment which had made running a chore I didn't do it anymore. Nearly 10 years later, realizing that there were times when I ran for my own goals and purposes and actually enjoyed the process I have embarked on the journey of a lifetime. Not only to reclaim my joy of running, but also to reclaim myself.

If you as I do, want to use running to improve sanity and health and connect with the amazing people that inhabit the streets of our fair city. From the wee hours of the morning, to the high suns of summer until the early twighlight of winter. Please follow me on my adventure and share in a few of the amazing races that are happening almost constantly in our great metropolis.

Looking forward to meeting you, ~ Troy
